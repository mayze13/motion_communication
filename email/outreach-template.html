Minds in Motion

We are opening recruitment for Minds in Motion — the next chapter in UCL's landmark programme of research into how the built environment shapes human behaviour, thought, and wellbeing.

In September 2024, 100 Minds in Motion brought more than one hundred participants into UCL PEARL — one of Europe's most advanced human behaviour research facilities — to take part in a groundbreaking neuroarchitecture experiment. Using moveable architectural partitions, full-body motion capture, wearable biosensors (EEG, heart rate, eye tracking, and cortisol sampling), and overhead camera tracking across 40,000 m² of controllable space, the study examined how spatial configuration shapes the way people move, navigate, and respond physiologically to their environment. The study was featured by MIT and UCL News.

Minds in Motion, running in September 2026, is the follow-up. Led by Professor Hugo Spiers (UCL Experimental Psychology) and Dr Fiona Zisch (UCL Bartlett School of Architecture), this new study builds on the original's findings to investigate fresh questions about how we experience and navigate architectural space — with real implications for how buildings are designed.

What participation involves:

▸ Location: UCL PEARL, Dagenham, London — a unique 40,000 m² controllable indoor research facility.
▸ Navigate a specially configured architectural space while wearing lightweight, non-invasive biosensors. No specialist knowledge or fitness level required.
▸ Open to all adults aged 18+, including neurodivergent individuals and wheelchair users. Full details on duration and scheduling provided on confirmation.
✦  A follow-up to 100 Minds in Motion — as featured by MIT and UCL News, September 2024.
